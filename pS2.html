Overture

A thirst for undying glory and power. A legacy to honor. A knawing guilt of duty. A secret to protect. The dying Republic of Kah is gasping for breath as it struggles to quiet the unease bubbling at the surface of the society it created. Rigid in its committment to let the pillars it built be demolished, Kah will crush any opposition to its Republican sham. Overture is a coming of age young adult novel set 9000 years in the future. Chronicling the lives of two sisters and their twofriends, as they grow into adults, they see and experience of the painful beauty, the awe inspiring terror of Kah. They will fight for everything they are to come out on the right side of history as one of the sister's, Sei, technological innovations proves to upheave the world as she knows it.

Overture came in a single pinnacle of clarity on a bleak November night at a Porter Robinson concert. The arena and people melted away, replaced by plastic, electrocardiagram mountains. They pulsed with each beat. A rainbow tinged hand outstretched and I took it, leaping into a dimmension where limitation and constraints vanished. It was a place with a technicolor tornado of a billion shining lights that smelled purple. The surreal beauty of life was revealed. Spiritual. There's no other explanation.

Crippling, overwhelming fear died then. Renewed commitment, discipline, passion replaced it. After that I was done with being in the comforting lull of mediocrity and complacency. Who was I? Who did I want to be? I wanted more for myself. I wanted to be more than I was, better than I ever was. So, in the spring semester of my senior year of college, I held my breath, rescinded my pharmacy school offers, and explored. I put myself first and actively pursued happiness. I coded, something I didn't think that I was capable of doing and enjoyed it in contrast to 4 years of physiology and dissections. Explored some more. Wrote. I lived in the moment. I was game for anything. Then, I had a website and a 100,000 word novel. Overture.
