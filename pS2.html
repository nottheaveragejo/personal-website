Overture

Overture came in a single pinnacle of clarity on a bleak November night at a Porter Robinson concert. The arena and people melted away, replaced by plastic, electrocardiagram mountains. They pulsed with each beat. A rainbow tinged hand outstretched and I took it, leaping into a dimmension where limitation and constraints came to die. It was a place with a technicolor tornado of a billion shining lights that smelled purple. The surreal beauty of life was revealed. Spiritual. There's no other explanation.

Crippling, overwhelming fear died at that momment. Renewed commitment, discipline, passion replaced it. After that night, I challenged myself to ask the hard questions. I saw life as more than a measure of titles and salary. It was then I swore I would not waste my years being a pharmacist. Who was I? Who did I want to be? I wanted more for myself. I wanted to be more than I was, better than I ever was. So, in the spring semester of my senior year of college, I held my breath and rescinded my pharmacy school offers and explored. I coded, something I didn't think that I was capable of doing and enjoyed it in contrast to 4 years of physiology and dissections. Explored some more. Wrote. Then I had a website I take great pride in and a 100,000 word novel. Overture.
